What is CodeRush?

The CodeRush is a team-based competition launched by INTECS where you get to discover your skills in coding and problem solving. During the course of this online two-day hackathon your goal is to gather your team and brainstorm to untangle the solutions for the enigmas presented to you. All the challenges you need to go through are published in the Hackerrank platform and they are set by our team of exceptional problem setters. This mesmerising event is exclusive for the members of FIT family. So don't miss out on this amazing opportunity. May the best team win!

Fun

This competition is a great instance for you to have fun with your friends while competing against them toe to toe.

Coding

Ensure you grab this chance to be victorious by coding with your favourite language. Let your passion of coding take you to a higher level.

How it works

Assemble a team ,and get registered for CodeRush. Once this is done, you will be assigned with Hackerrank accounts (one per each team) and you will receive further information via emails. An awareness session will take place so that you can better understand the competition’s procedure and ready your game-plan The practice playground will be then open for you to warm up, and get your team all strategized for the upcoming event. The competition will commence afterwards, where together with your team, you will be competing to tackle the puzzles presented to you.

The following are the ground rules for CodeRush 2020

Rules and Regulations

CodeRush 2020 is exclusive for the undergraduates of the Faculty of Information Technology.


The competition will be in action for 48 hours from the starting moment.


A team can only log into Hackerrank utilising the account and password sent by the Organising Committee.

The competition consists of 15 questions and an ideathon / mini-ideathon, with the questions’ complexity ranging from easy to medium and hard.

The points earned through Hackerrank will be accounted for 70% of total marks while 30% will be calculated through the marks earned for the ideathon. (It should be taken to notice that the evaluation of the submissions for the ideathon will be conducted by a panel of impartial panel of non-student judges)

A team can submit only one answer per given question.

The teams’ rankings are done in accordance with the number of problems solved and the time taken for each.

A participant can be a member of one and one team only.

The Contest Committee will be tallying each round’s marks of each team and publish them.

The Contest Committee’s decisions on all matters will be final.

Any violations of rules committed before or during the competition will lead the team towards ineligibility for the final prizes.

Contact the Organising Committee of CodeRush 2020 for further information.
